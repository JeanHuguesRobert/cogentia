## Abstract

Cogentia Commons is a method and infrastructure for the traceable exploration of possibilities. Its default regime is public, because public traces are criticizable, reusable, forkable, transmissible, and resistant to capture.

However, public-by-default does not mean public-only. Some explorations require private or restricted spaces: sensitive archives, personal or institutional records, preparatory work, community-restricted inquiry, invention-oriented research, patent strategy, trade-secret assessment, or publication-deferral decisions.

These private spaces are not outside Cogentia Commons. They are **visibility modes** of Cogentia Commons. The method remains the same: traces, hypotheses, objections, bifurcations, continuations, decisions, source documents, and derived products. What changes is access, timing, publication policy, backend sovereignty, and risk handling.

The economic principle is explicit: **private uses should finance, harden, and maintain the public commons**. The private mode is legitimate when it protects persons, preserves strategic value, enables future publication, or subsidizes the shared infrastructure without redefining the public commons around private capture.

---

## 1. Core thesis

> **Cogentia Commons is public by default and private by exception. Private modes are visibility modes of Cogentia Commons, not separate product cores.**

This thesis has three parts:

1. **Product core.** Cogentia Commons is the central product and method for exploration.
2. **Visibility rule.** Public visibility is the default because it maximizes criticism, reuse, auditability, and anti-capture.
3. **Private exception.** Private or restricted visibility is legitimate when immediate publication would harm persons, destroy value, compromise patentability, expose sensitive traces, or prevent responsible exploration.

The distinction is not:

```text
Cogentia Core vs Cogentia Commons vs Cogentia Patents
```

It is:

```text
Cogentia Commons
  public mode by default
  private mode by exception
  patents / R&D mode as one private or restricted community use case
```

---

## 2. Public by default

Cogentia Commons is public by default because public traces make knowledge work more robust.

Publicity allows:

- criticism;
- reuse;
- forkability;
- external correction;
- transmission;
- publication of source documents;
- production of derived products;
- audit of conceptual genealogy;
- resistance to hidden capture;
- independent continuation by future humans or agents.

The public regime is therefore not a mere publication preference. It is a methodological and political choice.

> Publicity is the default anti-capture posture of Cogentia Commons.

This does not imply that every conversation, private trace, archive, preparatory note, or sensitive document should be published. The conversation is an atelier; the corpus receives stabilized material.

---

## 3. Private by exception

Private visibility is justified when public exposure would damage the very possibility of responsible exploration.

Legitimate cases include:

| Case | Reason for private or restricted mode |
|---|---|
| Personal archives | Protection of persons, privacy, sensitivity, dignity, legal prudence. |
| Institutional registers | Evidence, continuity, accountability, controlled access. |
| Preparatory work | Avoid premature publication of unstable or misleading fragments. |
| Community-restricted inquiry | Allow a bounded group to explore before public stabilization. |
| R&D exploration | Preserve strategic options while keeping internal traceability. |
| Patent-oriented exploration | Avoid destroying patentability or strategic value before decision. |
| Trade-secret assessment | Decide whether secrecy, patent, defensive publication, or public release is appropriate. |
| Publication deferral | Prepare a public document without exposing incomplete or risky material too early. |

Private mode is therefore not an anti-commons stance. It is an access and timing regime.

> Private mode exists to protect exploration until the right publication, disclosure, filing, or abandonment decision can be made.

---

## 4. The patents mode

The term **Cogentia Patents** should be understood as shorthand for:

> **the patent-oriented private or restricted community mode of Cogentia Commons.**

It is not a separate core product.

Its purpose is not to mechanically produce patents. Its purpose is to help a community or enterprise explore technical possibilities, trace hypotheses, identify inventive features, record objections, and decide between several exits:

- patent filing;
- trade secret;
- defensive publication;
- public source document;
- restricted memo;
- suspension;
- abandonment with trace.

A patent is ambivalent. During its protection period, it restricts use. Over time, it may also become part of the public technical corpus, especially when it expires or when its content is reused as disclosed knowledge. In this sense, patent-oriented work can be treated as a **potential commons under temporary restriction**, not as a pure negation of commons.

This must remain a cautious formulation. Cogentia Commons does not romanticize patents. It treats them as one possible legal and economic routing of an invention packet.

---

## 5. Private does not mean non-public forever

A private exploration may later produce public artifacts.

Possible transitions:

```text
private trace → public source document
private R&D note → patent filing
private technical branch → defensive publication
private archive → redacted historical document
private community review → public working paper
private register → public proof summary
private invention path → abandoned trace kept internally
```

The question is not whether a trace is public or private once and for all.

The better question is:

> What is the legitimate visibility state of this trace at this stage of the exploration?

Visibility becomes a stateful property:

```text
private → restricted → embargoed → redacted → public
```

or, in some cases:

```text
public → corrected → deprecated → superseded
```

## 6. `registre-mariani` as precedent

The private repository `JeanHuguesRobert/registre-mariani` is an existing precedent for controlled registry logic.

It should be cited only at the structural level:

- it is a private repository;
- it demonstrates that the corpus already uses controlled Git spaces;
- it can serve as a precedent for private registers, sensitive archives, preparatory traces, or restricted explorations;
- its contents should not be inferred, summarized, or exposed merely because the repository is named.

The lesson is architectural:

> A public corpus may coexist with private registers when the boundary is explicit, traceable, and governed.

This precedent is useful for Cogentia Commons because it prevents a false opposition between open public work and controlled private spaces.

---

## 7. Private uses finance the public commons

The economic rule is:

> **Private uses finance, harden, and maintain the public commons.**

This rule matters because an open infrastructure still has costs:

- development;
- maintenance;
- review;
- hosting;
- backups;
- security;
- documentation;
- migration;
- legal prudence;
- user support;
- sovereign deployment options.

Some communities and organizations have stronger needs:

- private repositories;
- access control;
- sovereign hosting;
- audit trails;
- legal review;
- patent workflow support;
- redaction workflows;
- backup and mirror policies;
- export guarantees.

These needs can justify paid services. Those services should not redefine the public commons around private clients. They should subsidize the common infrastructure.

---

## 8. Backend sovereignty gradient

Private mode does not imply a single technical backend.

A visibility mode may use different sovereignty levels:

| Level | Backend example | Suitable for |
|---|---|---|
| Public GitHub | public GitHub repository | Default public corpus and open critique. |
| GitHub private | private GitHub repository | Lightweight restricted work, early-stage private registers. |
| GitLab / Forgejo / Gitea private | self-hosted or organization-hosted forge | Stronger control, institutional autonomy. |
| Client-controlled sovereign stack | dedicated private infrastructure | sensitive R&D, patent strategy, legal risk, strategic records. |
| Offline/local-first mirror | local repositories and backups | sensitive archives, resilience, pre-publication staging. |

The method should not be confused with GitHub itself.

Git is one substrate. GitHub is one service. Cogentia Commons is the method and infrastructure discipline for traceable exploration.

---

## 9. Governance requirements for private modes

Every private mode should define at least:

1. **Visibility policy** — who can see what, when, and why.
2. **Access policy** — who can read, write, review, approve, export, mirror, or delete.
3. **Publication policy** — when private material may become public, redacted, summarized, or remain private.
4. **IP policy** — for invention-oriented work: patent, secret, defensive publication, public release, or abandonment.
5. **Backup and mirror policy** — how continuity and sovereignty are preserved.
6. **Exit policy** — how a community can recover its corpus if the hosting service changes or fails.
7. **Human validation anchors** — what decisions require an explicit living human decision.
8. **Non-disclosure boundary** — what may be named publicly without exposing contents.

Private mode without governance becomes a hidden silo. Private mode with explicit governance can become a controlled incubation layer for future public knowledge.

## 12. Objections and safeguards

### Objection 1 — Private mode may capture the commons

Risk: commercial clients may gradually redefine the public infrastructure around private needs.

Response: the doctrine must remain explicit. Private services finance, harden, and maintain the commons. They do not become the sovereign purpose of Cogentia Commons.

Safeguard:

```text
public doctrine > private client convenience
```

### Objection 2 — Patent mode contradicts commons

Risk: patents restrict use and can become anti-competitive instruments.

Response: the patent mode must not romanticize patents. It treats patenting as one possible routing decision among patent, secret, defensive publication, public release, suspension, and abandonment.

Safeguard: every patent-oriented workflow should preserve the decision record explaining why patenting is preferable to immediate publication or defensive publication.

### Objection 3 — Private repositories may become invisible knowledge graves

Risk: sensitive or strategic material may remain private forever, becoming unusable and untransmissible.

Response: private mode should include publication, redaction, summary, export, and continuation policies.

Safeguard: private does not mean ungoverned. It means controlled visibility.

### Objection 4 — Naming `registre-mariani` may expose a sensitive perimeter

Risk: merely naming a private repository might invite curiosity or incorrect inference.

Response: the document only cites `registre-mariani` as an architectural precedent. It does not describe contents.

Safeguard: cite structure, not substance.

### Objection 5 — Too much visibility machinery may bureaucratize the method

Risk: visibility states, governance policies and publication decisions could make Cogentia Commons too heavy.

Response: the pipeline remains a default route, not a checklist. Small communities may use simple policies; sensitive or patent-oriented communities need stronger policies.

Safeguard: proportionality.

- Private modes are best understood as visibility modes, not separate product cores.
- Patent-oriented exploration is one private or restricted community use case of Cogentia Commons.

### Level C — Product and economic hypothesis

- Paid private use cases can finance, harden, and maintain the public commons.
- Patent-oriented communities may become a commercially viable use case for FractaVolta or a related operator.
- The same source methodology can serve open commons and restricted communities if visibility and governance are explicit.

### Level D — Open design questions

- Which private modes require GitHub private repositories, and which require more sovereign hosting?
- What is the minimal patent-oriented workflow before involving a patent attorney or industrial-property counsel?
- How should private material be summarized, redacted, or converted into public source documents?
- How should the platform prevent private-client priorities from distorting the public commons?
