# Cogentia Commons: A Platform Architecture for Collaborative Possibility Exploration Under Scientific Constraint

## Abstract
Cogentia Commons is proposed as a public-by-default infrastructure for collaborative exploration of ideas, theories, projects, and research programs under explicit scientific constraints. The platform does not claim to determine truth; rather, it organizes disciplined conjecture, critique, revision, and synthesis through persistent traceability. It operationalizes thesis kernels, epistemic status tagging, multi-agent critique loops, and recognition signals decoupled from validity claims. We define a minimal architecture, discuss governance and failure modes, and propose an empirical evaluation plan. The core claim is procedural: “The platform distributes the conditions of exploration; it does not democratize truth.”

## 1. Introduction
Scientific and civic systems both struggle with exploratory work that is too early for canonical publication but too consequential for unstructured discourse. Cogentia Commons addresses this gap by structuring exploratory reasoning while preserving revisability.

## 2. Conceptual Background
### Possibilism
Possibilism motivates systematic exploration of plausible but unproven pathways.
### Adjacent Possible
The platform expands near-reachable conceptual options through critique loops.
### Correctability
All claims remain revisable with visible objections and revision history.
### Commons Governance
Participation is structured as a knowledge commons with anti-capture design.
### Personal Cogentia
A personal reasoning trace becomes composable in collaborative settings.
### Epistemic Traceability
Each premise, objection, revision, and synthesis is addressable and auditable.

## 3. Problem Statement
Institutions over-filter high-uncertainty ideas; public discourse conflates opinion and knowledge; LLMs enable new workflows; publishing remains static and low-feedback.

## 4. Platform Theory
Thesis Kernel, Cogentia Graph, Multi-Agent Critique Loop, Epistemic Status Layer, Recognition Commons, and donation-backed reputation compose the theoretical core.

## 5. Architecture
Entity model includes User, CogentiaProfile, Thesis, Premise, Reference, Constraint, Claim, Objection, Revision, AgentReview, Artifact, DonationSignal, ReputationScore, and DeliberationThread.

## 6. Recursive First Use Case
“The first object explored by Cogentia Commons is Cogentia Commons itself.” The platform hosts iterative versions of this paper with explicit objections.

## 7. Risks and Failure Modes
Key risks include epistemic populism, gaming, plutocratic capture, hallucination contamination, privacy confusion, cultic closure, and over-modeling.

## 8. Evaluation Plan
Compare artifact quality pre/post critique loop, track accepted objections, reference diversity, correction latency, and researcher-centered usability studies.

## 9. Conclusion
Cogentia Commons should be interpreted as exploratory infrastructure, not truth arbitration. “Donations signal recognition, not validity.”
