# Individual and Collective Digital Twins

## A dialectic between sovereign twins of natural persons and of legal persons

## Abstract

The existing Cogentia twin family — [The Sovereign Digital Twin](cogentia-digital-twin.md), [Cogentia and Cogentigrams](Cogentia-and-Cogentigram.md), [Structural Signatures](structural_signatures.md), [Cogentigraphic Distillation](cogentigraphic_distillation.md) — formalises the idea of a *sovereign digital twin* of a person: a governed interpretive structure operating on a traceable, multi-source corpus, distinct from an archive that speaks for itself, from a retrieval index, and from a simulation.

This note extends that family by formalising a **dialectic** between two sub-classes that the existing papers treat only implicitly: the twin of a **natural person** (*personne physique*) and the twin of a **legal person** (*personne morale*). The two share a substrate but diverge in object, in legal regime, in ethical risk and in operational governance.

Working thesis:

> A faithful digital twin is not an archive that speaks. It is a governed interpretive structure operating on a traceable multi-source corpus.
>
> The twin of a natural person protects an embodied memory.
> The twin of a legal person traces a collective capacity.
>
> The first guards against *usurpation*. The second guards against *irresponsibility*.
> Both require a governed multi-source corpus.

---

## 1. The sovereign digital twin

A *sovereign digital twin* (cf [`cogentia-digital-twin.md`](cogentia-digital-twin.md)) is the right and infrastructure for an entity to own and govern the structured interpretation of its own traces, instead of having that interpretation produced opaquely by an external AI deployer. Its measurable form is the **Cogentigram** ([`Cogentia-and-Cogentigram.md`](Cogentia-and-Cogentigram.md)) — a structured representation along defined axes, with uncertainty estimation and graded disclosure.

[`structural_signatures.md`](structural_signatures.md) develops a key methodological move: the twin's identifying axis is *not* a biometric or biographical record but a **non-biographical structural signature**, consent-based, contractually governed. [`cogentigraphic_distillation.md`](cogentigraphic_distillation.md) refines this further by **separating cognitive operating rules** (style of action, decision heuristics) **from biographical and factual memory** (events, facts, names), so an agent grounded in the twin acts *in the manner of* the subject without claiming to *be* the subject.

The dialectic this paper introduces is orthogonal to those refinements: it asks who the subject *is* — a natural person or a legal person — and what changes when the answer changes.

---

## 2. Natural person and legal person

Both can be the subject of a sovereign digital twin. Both are recognised as *persons* in law and in social practice. But they differ at every layer:

| Axis | Natural person | Legal person |
|---|---|---|
| Substrate | embodied memory | acts, mandates, decisions |
| Continuity | mortal, posthumous regime | indefinite, transferable, dissolvable |
| Will | individual consent | collegial governance, statutes |
| Risk | usurpation, simulation, dignity | irresponsibility, opacity, capture |
| Sources | private, semi-public, intimate | public, recorded, traceable |
| Default disclosure | restricted | public when imputable |
| Personification | constrained | structurally forbidden |

The twin family applies to both, but the *governance regime*, the *régime de preuve*, and the *régime éthique* differ.

---

## 3. The twin of a natural person

The components flagged by the existing papers — Cogentigram axes, structural signature, distilled cognitive operating rules — are *necessary* but not *sufficient*. To be faithful, the twin of a natural person must also articulate:

- **embodied memory** — the corpus is not a brain; it is a curated trace of acts, words, works;
- **identity** — non-biographical structural signature plus a minimal biographical anchor;
- **intimacy** — what stays private, even within the corpus;
- **work** (*œuvre*) — what was published, authored, performed;
- **consent** — graded, revocable, expressible by the subject (or, posthumously, by rights holders within strict limits);
- **non-usurpation** — the twin acts *in the style of* the subject; it does not impersonate, does not claim, does not decide on the subject's behalf.

The faithful twin is consultative, not declarative. It can be *queried* about how the subject would have reasoned about something; it must not be *enacted as* the subject.

---

## 4. The posthumous case: Marie-Louise

Marie-Louise stands here as the founding case of a posthumous twin of a natural person who was also an author of works (*œuvre*).

The posthumous case sharpens every constraint of §3 and adds new ones:

- **droit moral** — in French law, the moral right over the work persists after death, transmissible to heirs but inalienable in essence;
- **ayants droit** — heirs exercise the moral right under fiduciary duty; an authorisation by heirs is *not* an unlimited license to make the deceased speak;
- **intimacy of the dead** — protected by jurisprudence and decency, independently of the heirs' wishes;
- **protection of third parties** — letters, photographs, testimonies frequently implicate persons who never consented to the twin;
- **prohibition of artificial resurrection** — the twin does not generate new "statements by" the deceased; it does not personify, ventriloquise or perform;
- **prohibition of simulation abusive** — even consensual personification by heirs is a category error if the simulation purports to *be* the subject rather than *represent traces of* the subject.

Compact formula:

> The posthumous twin is not a resurrection. It is a device of governed memory.

A faithful posthumous twin is consultative, attributive (it always points back to the source), and conservative. Its productions are clearly second-order: *« on the basis of the surviving corpus, the subject's voice on this topic shows the following invariants »*, never *« the subject says »*.

---

## 5. The twin of a legal person

A legal person — association, foundation, company, public body — is a fiction of law that *acts*, *contracts*, *answers*, and *endures*. Its twin is therefore not consultative-only; it must be *operationally traceable*.

Its corpus components:

- **statutes** and amendments;
- **mandates** — current officers, durations, scope;
- **decisions** — minutes, resolutions, ballots;
- **acts** — outbound and inbound correspondence, contracts, public communications;
- **projects** — what the entity does, with which partners, on which timeline;
- **capacity** — what the entity is *able* to do (cf the Cogentia notion of *Autonomie de Capacité*);
- **responsibility chain** — who answers for what, when, under which mandate.

This connects directly to the [`traceabilite_des_actes`](https://github.com/JeanHuguesRobert/barons-Mariani/blob/main/research/traceabilite_des_actes.md) doctrine: the twin of a legal person makes that doctrine operational at the entity scale.

Compact formula:

> The twin of a legal person does not simulate a consciousness. It renders traceable a continuity of acts, mandates and capacities.

The risk it guards against is not usurpation but **dilution of responsibility** — the institutional opacity in which decisions have no identifiable author and acts have no chain of imputability.

---

## 6. The institutional case: C.O.R.S.I.C.A.

C.O.R.S.I.C.A. — *Corse Organisant la Réunion Sur Internet de Compétences Autonomes*, founded in Corte in late 1995 — is the founding case of a twin of a legal person in this corpus. It is an associative (loi 1901) entity, currently presided by the present author.

Its corpus today includes:

- statutes and registration trace;
- GitHub organisation and personal repositories carrying its public doctrine (cogentia, barons-Mariani, marenostrum, …);
- email correspondence (Gmail, archives Yahoo to be recovered via [`mailarch`](https://github.com/JeanHuguesRobert/cogentia/issues/11));
- the *Interaction Packets* register ([`JeanHuguesRobert/interaction_packets/dashboard.md`](https://github.com/JeanHuguesRobert/JeanHuguesRobert/blob/main/interaction_packets/dashboard.md)) — outbound démarches and their replies;
- audit trail (`.cogentia/audit.jsonl`) for the operational pipeline;
- doctrine documents in the cogentia and barons-Mariani research surfaces.

C.O.R.S.I.C.A. as a legal person has a defined act of foundation, a continuous mandate, accumulated decisions and projects, and a public surface. Its twin makes all of that *queryable*, *imputable*, *contestable*. The twin's operational test: can a third party reconstruct, from the corpus alone, the chain *act → mandate → decision → outcome*?

## 7. The individual / collective dialectic

Bringing §3-4 and §5-6 face to face:

```text
twin of a natural person      twin of a legal person
─────────────────────────     ─────────────────────────
protects embodied memory      traces collective capacity
guards against usurpation     guards against irresponsibility
consultative, attributive     operational, imputable
personification constrained   personification forbidden
default: restricted           default: public when imputable
sources: private + œuvre      sources: acts + records
posthumous regime applies     dissolution regime applies
ayants droit                  officers, mandataires
```

The dialectic is not a binary. A natural person *holds* mandates in legal persons; a legal person *is acted by* natural persons. Their twins therefore touch and inform each other. The author of this paper is simultaneously the subject of a personal twin and the president of C.O.R.S.I.C.A. Some émanations of the same act (e.g., a letter to elected officials) belong to *both* twins, under different regimes.

What the dialectic forbids is the *confusion* of regimes — letting a personal posthumous consultation acquire institutional weight, or letting an institutional act dissolve into a personal narrative.

---

## 8. Multi-source corpus and régimes de preuve

The corpus that grounds either twin is multi-source. Each source carries a *régime de preuve* — a degree to which its contents can be opposed as evidence:

| Source | Régime de preuve | Role for the twin |
|---|---|---|
| GitHub (public commits, issues, doctrine) | strong public trace | primary substrate for legal-person twins |
| Email / IMAP (private, dated) | strong private trace | primary substrate for natural-person twins, posthumous archives |
| Supabase / Postgres (structured + audit) | strong structured trace | workflows, audit logs, governance state |
| Buckets / S3 (heavy artefacts) | strong, with hash | works, attachments, cold archives |
| RAG / vector store | **not a canonical source** | semantic projection over the canonical sources |
| Markdown / YAML packets | strong human-readable trace | derived products, continuations, decision artefacts |
| Œuvres (artistic works) | author-attested trace | core substance for posthumous twins of authors |
| Témoignages | relational, prudent | usable under heightened care, never alone |

The twin is *not* identical with any one source. It is the **governed interpretive structure** that selects, weighs, dates and discloses traces from these sources under a stated régime.

## 9. Archive, corpus, RAG, twin, simulation — distinctions

The five terms are routinely confused. The corpus's doctrine requires them to remain distinct:

```text
raw archive       — undifferentiated trace, no governance
governed corpus   — versioned, attributable, contestable
RAG index         — semantic projection over a corpus, not a source
digital twin      — governed interpretive structure operating on a corpus
free simulation   — unconstrained generation; can become usurpation
```

The error to avoid:

- treating a raw archive *as if* it were a governed corpus;
- treating a RAG retrieval *as if* it were a source;
- treating a twin's output *as if* it were the subject's statement;
- treating a free simulation *as if* it had any traceable relation to the subject.

Each transition between layers must be explicit, governed and traceable.

## 10. Operational consequences for mailarch, GitHub, Supabase, buckets, RAG

The same tool can serve both regimes, but the regime must be **declared per subject**. The minimum metadata an issue-tracker like [`mailarch`](https://github.com/JeanHuguesRobert/cogentia/issues/11) should carry on each packet:

```yaml
subject:
  type: physical_person | legal_person
  name: ""
  posthumous: true | false
  rights_context: heirs | officers | author | unknown
  redaction_default: true | false
  publication_default: true | false
  personification_allowed: false   # always, until further notice
```

Consequences per tool:

- **mailarch (issue #11)** — IMAP extraction always honours the per-subject regime; never bulk-publishes; produces governed packets, not raw dumps.
- **GitHub** — primary substrate of legal-person twins; for natural persons, only the consented public surface (commits, repos, public profile) is twin-eligible.
- **Supabase / Postgres** — every record carries actor + timestamp + mandate (the audit invariants from `traceabilite_des_actes`).
- **Buckets** — heavy artefacts hash-indexed; access logged; never the *canonical* source for the twin (only an artefact store).
- **RAG / vector store** — explicitly *not* a source. RAG outputs always carry attribution back to canonical packets; a twin must never answer purely from RAG retrieval without source resolution.

The same régime applies recursively to the twin's own outputs: a twin's statement is itself an act, traceable, attributable, contestable.

## 11. Open questions

- What exact role for *ayants droit* in a posthumous twin? Where does fiduciary duty end and authorisation begin?
- What hard limits on personification, beyond the general prohibition?
- How to distinguish consultative memory from abusive simulation in borderline cases (e.g., reconstructing a missing letter)?
- What future role for a memorial fund (*Les Amis de Malou*) as a fiduciary structure for a posthumous twin?
- What role for C.O.R.S.I.C.A. as the legal-person host of operational decisions about both classes of twins?
- What articulation with the Fondation Barons Mariani — patrimonial vs operational role?
- Which corpus layers can become public? Which must stay private / familial / restricted?
- How to *contest*, *suspend*, or *correct* a digital twin once it operates?
- How to articulate droit moral, droit patrimonial, family memory and technical governance — without one layer silently dominating the others?

A derived product, more public-facing and less technical, is anticipated as `barons-Mariani/research/memoire_transmission_jumeaux_numeriques.md` — addressing the family / patrimonial / cultural dimension of the same dialectic.
