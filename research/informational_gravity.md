# Informational Gravity

## Contextual Attraction for Fractanet Packet Routing

## 1. Status and purpose

This document is the proposed sovereign source for **informational gravity** (`gravité
informationnelle`) in Fractanet.

It answers one question:

> How can a cognitive packet be routed toward mobilizable capacities that are likely, legitimate,
> and currently able to advance it toward its expected destination?

The concept belongs to Cogentia because it concerns cognition and cognitive-packet routing across
potentially competing Fractanet implementations. FractaVolta is one private company that may build
Fractanet- and COP-compliant networks; it is not the sovereign owner of the general concept. COP
supplies its trace, evidence, projection, and conformance mechanics. Inseme currently hosts both the
COP specification and its first reference implementation; a future separation or migration of the
specification toward Cogentia should be kept in mind without forcing an urgent move. A runtime such
as Inox may execute an algorithm, but no company or implementation silently defines the concept.

This is a working source. It does not claim that the metaphor establishes a new law of physics, nor
that one universal formula has already been discovered.

## 3. Core intuition

Physical gravity makes bodies move according to a field produced by masses and distances.
Informational gravity uses that image carefully: packets appear to be **attracted** toward capacities
whose mobilization offers a credible path of useful progress.

In Fractanet, a capable human, agent, node, tool, repository, institution, or collective may attract
a packet because it combines several situated properties:

- relevant competence;
- current availability and capacity;
- legitimacy and mandate;
- evidence of useful prior acts;
- contextual reputation;
- proximity to the packet's expected destination;
- acceptable cost, latency, uncertainty, and risk.

None of these properties is sufficient alone. A famous actor without availability, a powerful node
without mandate, or a cheap handler unable to advance the packet MUST NOT acquire decisive gravity.

## 4. Definition and naming

### 4.1 Informational gravity

**Informational gravity is the contextual, time-bounded, traceable attraction exerted by a
mobilizable capacity on a packet, as estimated from that capacity's legitimate ability to advance
the packet toward its expected destination under the applicable constraints.**

It is a relation:

```text
informational_gravity(packet, candidate_capacity, context, time)
```

It is not an intrinsic scalar permanently attached to a person or node. **Attraction** is retained
as the tangible term for the comparative effect perceived by a router or user.

### 4.2 Packet

`Packet` has the meaning defined by the applicable packet source. For the primary case considered
here, it is a [cognitive packet](https://github.com/JeanHuguesRobert/cogentia/blob/main/research/cognitive_packets.md)
carrying an envelope and a payload.

### 4.3 Mobilizable capacity

A **mobilizable capacity** is an identified human or technical capability that:

1. exists or is credibly announced;
2. is reachable under the current regime;
3. is willing or authorized to accept the relevant packet class;
4. has enough available resources to perform a bounded next act;
5. can produce an inspectable continuation, result, refusal, or failure trace.

Latent competence without a route, availability, authority, or response contract is not yet a
mobilizable capacity.

### 4.4 Expected destination

An **expected destination** is a desired future state of the packet, not necessarily a network
address and not necessarily a fully known final answer.

Examples include:

- obtain a verified answer;
- produce a lawful decision;
- repair an identified defect;
- reach a human competent to arbitrate;
- acquire missing evidence;
- transform a hypothesis into a tested result;
- return a safe and explicit failure.

The destination is **expected** because cognitive work is uncertain. Routing may revise, refine,
branch, or reject the initial destination, but such changes MUST be traced.

### 4.5 Attraction

**Attraction** is a comparative routing estimate. It answers:

> Among the presently eligible capacities, which ones offer the strongest justified expectation of
> useful progress for this packet?

Attraction is not coercion. A capacity may refuse, expire, become unavailable, or impose a price.
A packet may also carry constraints that prohibit an otherwise attractive route.

## 5. Packet Attractor and informational gravity

The two concepts are complementary.

| Packet Attractor | Informational gravity |
|---|---|
| Declaration made by or about a capacity | Contextual relation between one packet and one candidate |
| “I am ready and legitimate to receive packets matching these conditions” | “This candidate may advance this packet under these conditions” |
| Supplies current demand, capability, mandate, and pressure information | Combines eligible evidence into a comparative routing field |
| May exist before a particular packet arrives | Exists only relative to a packet, context, and time |

A Packet Attractor is important evidence, but it MUST NOT self-certify its own gravitational weight.
Self-declared capacity must be combined with independent traces, resource availability, mandate,
constraints, and competing alternatives.

## 6. Field model

Informational gravity is best represented as a **vector of named factors plus a governed comparison
function**, not as an unexplained global score.

For packet `p`, candidate `c`, context `x`, and time `t`, an implementation may compute:

\[
G(p,c,x,t) = E(p,c,x,t) \times A(p,c,x,t)
\]

where:

- \(E\) is an eligibility gate: zero when a hard constraint fails, one when the route is eligible;
- \(A\) is a contextual attraction function over disclosed positive and negative factors.

One possible family is:

\[
A = F(C, V, L, P, R, Q) - H(K, D, U, X)
\]

| Symbol | Factor | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| \(C\) | capability fit | Match between required and offered capabilities |
| \(V\) | availability | Current resources, queue, attention, and response horizon |
| \(L\) | legitimacy | Identity, mandate, jurisdiction, consent, and policy compliance |
| \(P\) | expected progress | Evidence that the candidate can reduce distance to the expected destination |
| \(R\) | contextual reputation | Relevant, time-bounded evidence of prior acts, including eligible Kudos traces |
| \(Q\) | continuity affinity | Ability to preserve context, provenance, and packet lineage |
| \(K\) | resource cost | Money, Kudos, energy, compute, time, bandwidth, or other budget consumption |
| \(D\) | delay | Expected latency and deadline risk |
| \(U\) | uncertainty and failure risk | Poor evidence, volatility, irreversibility, or low confidence |
| \(X\) | capture exposure | Concentration, dependency, conflicts of interest, manipulation, and lock-in |

The formula is a grammar, not a mandated universal equation. **Traceability is the only universal
mandatory factor.** The other factors are available dimensions that a routing policy MAY use. When
they are unavailable, a router SHOULD seek an explicit degraded route instead of inventing evidence
or stopping without an inspectable failure trace.

Each routing profile MUST publish:

- the factors it uses;
- units, normalization, and missing-data treatment;
- hard constraints and weights or comparison rules;
- time windows and decay rules;
- algorithm and policy versions;
- tie-breaking, exploration, and fallback policy.

## 7. Reputation and Kudos

Reputation is one possible contribution to informational gravity. It is neither the whole field nor
an intrinsic moral truth.

Kudos may contribute distinctive evidence because, under the
[Kudos doctrine](https://github.com/JeanHuguesRobert/barons-Mariani/blob/main/research/kudos.md),
the gift is public, voluntary, nominative, and traceable. It can therefore indicate that identified
users considered a provider worthy of recognition in connection with real activity.

For routing purposes:

- only Kudos relevant to the packet's context SHOULD contribute;
- the giver, mandate, community, age, circulation history, reversals, and disputes MAY affect the
  interpretation;
- delegated Kudos MUST distinguish the principal from the technical actor;
- repeated reciprocal, collusive, self-funded, or reputation-purchasing patterns MUST remain
  visible and MAY be discounted or excluded under a published policy;
- purchase details that are not necessary for the public proof MUST remain private;
- absence of Kudos MUST NOT prove incapacity or vice;
- a large Kudos balance MUST NOT automatically create routing priority.

Kudos are thus **one possible system of stigmergic routing evidence**, designed within this Corpus:
traces left by prior users may guide later users and packets. Other reputation systems may coexist
or later prove better. Kudos is neither the ultimate truth nor a mandatory source of attraction. It
does not replace capability verification, consent, budgets, mandates, or judgment.

## 8. Accounting and skin in the game

Informational gravity becomes manipulable when declarations are costless and failures have no
consequence. Fractanet therefore needs the rigorous resource and mandate accounting specified by
[COP/Accounting](https://github.com/JeanHuguesRobert/inseme/blob/main/packages/cop-core/COP_ACCOUNTING.md).

Before route commitment, the control plane SHOULD be able to establish:

- which budget funds the packet;
- which resources the candidate reserves or consumes;
- who acts and for which principal;
- which mandate authorizes the act;
- what reward, collateral, liability, or opportunity cost creates skin in the game;
- how success, partial progress, refusal, failure, dispute, and reversal will be recorded.

Skin in the game does not require reducing every consequence to money. Attention, compute, energy,
time, bounded reputation, collateral, repair duties, and explicit liability may all matter, provided
they are not conflated and their accounting rules are stated.

What matters to one subject may be insignificant to another, so no universal stake metric is safe.
The minimal common ground is preservation of life. A stronger practical horizon is **live and let
live**. Consequential policies SHOULD seek the positive Golden Rule: act toward others as one would
wish others to act toward oneself. This anchor is developed in
[Constitution minimale des communs](../docs/constitution_minimale_des_communs.md).

## 9. COP projection

COP MUST record the evidence and decision path without creating a second hidden routing truth.

### 9.1 Durable traces

The following facts SHOULD be COP Events or referenced Artifacts:

- packet envelope and expected destination;
- candidate attractor declarations;
- capability, availability, price, mandate, and policy evidence;
- accounting budget and reservation result;
- candidate set considered;
- algorithm and policy versions;
- factor vector for each eligible candidate, subject to justified disclosure limits;
- selected route and stated reason;
- refusal, progress, continuation, settlement, failure, dispute, or reversal.

### 9.2 Derived Views

The following are derived Views and MUST be reproducible from the authoritative trace:

- current candidate set;
- information-gravity vector or score;
- reputation inputs;
- available packet budget;
- route ranking;
- aggregate performance indicators.

An implementation MUST NOT preserve only the winning score. Auditors need enough evidence to
reconstruct why plausible alternatives were accepted, rejected, or never eligible.

## 10. Routing procedure

A minimal conforming router performs these stages:

1. **Read the packet** — identify required capabilities, expected destination, constraints, regime,
   deadline, privacy class, and budget.
2. **Discover candidates** — query current Packet Attractors and other governed registries.
3. **Apply hard gates** — reject invalid identity, mandate, consent, capability, policy, safety,
   availability, jurisdiction, and budget conditions.
4. **Build factor vectors** — retain evidence and uncertainty; do not convert missing evidence into
   invented certainty.
5. **Compare eligible routes** — apply the versioned policy, including exploration and anti-capture
   constraints.
6. **Reserve resources** — prevent double commitment before dispatch.
7. **Commit and trace** — record the choice, evidence, mandate, budget, and responsible principal.
8. **Observe progress** — settle, continue, reroute, dispute, or fail explicitly.
9. **Learn without rewriting history** — later evidence may update future projections, never past
   events.

## 11. Anti-capture and pluralism invariants

Informational gravity risks creating the equivalent of gravitational monopolies. A successful node
receives more packets, gains more traces and Kudos, and may then attract still more packets.

A conforming policy MUST therefore:

- keep reputation contextual rather than global;
- expose concentration and dependency metrics;
- distinguish earned evidence from self-declaration and sponsored visibility;
- permit new or low-history candidates to be explored within bounded risk;
- avoid making one proprietary scorer or registry a mandatory chokepoint;
- apply diversity, redundancy, jurisdictional, or community constraints when appropriate;
- support contestation and correction;
- degrade or suspend compromised signals without erasing their history;
- preserve human arbitration for consequential or ambiguous routes.

Governance SHOULD remain latent while routing works smoothly: citizens need not be continuously
mobilized. It becomes essential when failures, disputes, capture, concentration, or constitutional
questions arise. Escalation thresholds, emergency paths, responsible bodies, and return-to-normal
conditions MUST therefore be traceable before they are needed.

The strongest apparent attraction MUST NOT override a hard safety, mandate, privacy, budget, or
constitutional constraint.

## 12. Privacy and transparency

Routing accountability requires a presumption of publicity and auditability. Everything SHOULD be
public unless a specific protected interest justifies an exception.

Public and restricted projections SHOULD answer, at the appropriate disclosure level:

- which policy governed the route;
- which kinds of evidence mattered;
- who was responsible for the decision;
- whether the act was delegated;
- how to contest it;
- whether the selected capacity progressed, failed, or was replaced.

When defence, security, private life, or another legitimate protected interest requires secrecy,
the restriction MUST be explicit, justified as far as safe disclosure permits, scoped to identified
information and readers, reasonably time-bounded, reviewable, auditable, and lifted when its
justification expires.

Sensitive payloads, bank data, personal communications, and irrelevant purchase details MAY thus
remain protected. Redaction, access, extension, and declassification decisions are consequential
acts and SHOULD be traceable. See also
[Pathologie du secret](https://github.com/JeanHuguesRobert/barons-Mariani/blob/main/research/pathologie_du_secret.md).

## 13. Failure modes and non-claims

### 13.1 Popularity masquerading as competence

High visibility or many Kudos may be irrelevant to the packet. Contextual capability and expected
progress remain necessary.

### 13.2 Circular reputation

If routing creates reputation and reputation alone determines routing, the field becomes a capture
machine. Exploration, decay, independent evidence, and concentration controls are required.

### 13.3 False precision

A numerical score may hide contested assumptions. Factor vectors, uncertainty, sources, and policy
versions MUST remain inspectable.

### 13.4 Destination dogmatism

The expected destination may be wrong. A capable handler must be allowed to return a reasoned
objection, safer destination, or explicit failure.

### 13.5 Metaphor overreach

Informational gravity is an engineering and institutional metaphor. It does not claim inverse-square
behavior, conservation laws, physical mass, or universal mathematical necessity.

### 13.6 Autonomous moral ranking

The concept does not authorize a global social-credit system, secret scoring, or an algorithmic
judgment of human worth.

## 14. Minimal conformance tests

An implementation claiming this profile MUST demonstrate that:

- the same candidate has different attraction for packets requiring different capabilities;
- an ineligible candidate cannot win through reputation or price;
- an unavailable high-reputation node loses to an available capable node when the policy requires
  timely progress;
- duplicate events do not duplicate reputation or accounting effects;
- reversed, disputed, expired, or unauthorized evidence is treated by an explicit policy;
- a new candidate can receive bounded exploration traffic;
- the chosen route cites its evidence and algorithm version;
- full COP replay reconstructs the same candidate eligibility and ranking;
- private Kudos-related purchase details do not leak into the public routing explanation;
- excessive concentration triggers the declared anti-capture response;
- a route can return a contestable failure or revised destination without falsifying success.

## 15. Human arbitration recorded on 2026-07-20

The author has made the following sovereign decisions and orientations:

1. **Naming.** `Informational gravity` is retained as the concept; `attraction` is retained as the
   tangible and widely understandable relational effect.
2. **Sovereign location.** The source belongs in Cogentia, not FractaVolta. FractaVolta may build one
   commercial implementation among alternatives. Inseme remains the current COP and reference-
   implementation location; a future movement of the specification toward Cogentia is noted but is
   not urgent.
3. **Handler rather than permanent agent.** Naming remains intentionally revisable. Cognitive
   packets are more central than durable agents. `Handler` is a preferred direction because a
   handler may be ephemeral and substitutable, reducing vendor lock-in. A dedicated terminology
   decision is still needed before changing existing COP schemas.
4. **Universal factor.** Traceability is the only universally mandatory factor. Missing optional
   factors lead toward explicit degraded routing and fallback, not fabricated certainty.
5. **Governance by exception.** Citizens need not be mobilized while the system works smoothly.
   Governance must become effective when a problem, dispute, capture, or constitutional exception
   arises.
6. **Diplomacy remains plural.** No universal diplomatic solution is asserted. Communities and
   confederations need governed agreements and traces, but protocol machinery must not pretend to
   replace diplomacy. Sun Tzu's *The Art of War* is recorded as an important intellectual reference,
   not as a protocol specification.
7. **Publicity with bounded secrecy.** Acts and routes are public and auditable by default. Any
   secrecy, including defence secrecy, is explicit, justified to the safe extent, scoped,
   reviewable, and reasonably time-bounded.
8. **Kudos is plural, not absolute.** Kudos is one reputation system designed by the author. Other
   present or future systems may coexist or prove better.
9. **Skin in the game.** No universal stake has equal meaning for everyone. Preservation of life is
   the safest common ground; live-and-let-live is preferable; the positive Golden Rule supplies the
   ethical orientation.
10. **Evidence and narrative.** No fixed set of claims can be declared sufficient in advance.
    Additional proof, especially living examples, is generally better. Proofs do not have equal
    practical impact, because narrative and storytelling affect whether evidence can mobilize
    understanding and action.

These decisions close the corresponding questions. Empirical weights, degraded-routing profiles,
confederal diplomacy, Handler terminology, and evidence-presentation policies remain open work.

## 16. Canonical compression

> **Informational gravity is the contextual, time-bounded, traceable attraction exerted by a
> mobilizable capacity on a packet, according to its legitimate ability to advance that packet
> toward an expected destination under explicit constraints.**

Or, more operationally:

> **A packet should be attracted not by whoever is most visible, but by whoever can most credibly,
> legitimately, and presently help it advance.**
