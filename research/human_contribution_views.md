# Human Contribution Views

## Probatory derived products for AI-assisted invention, creation, and institutional accountability

## Status

This is a source document of the Cogentia corpus.

It formalizes a derived-product category needed by AI-assisted invention, creation, institutional accountability, PrivaAI governance, KYS profiles, and FractaVolta commercial services.

It is not legal advice. It is a conceptual, methodological and operational source document. Patent, copyright, trade-secret, data-protection, employment and corporate-law applications require jurisdiction-specific legal review.

---

## 1. Core thesis

A **Human Contribution View** is a probatory cumulative derived product extracted from a governed corpus to establish the real, substantial, dated, attributable and contestable contribution of living human contributors in an AI-assisted process.

It is not the source.

The source is the corpus.

A Human Contribution View is a structured view over that corpus, produced for a specific probatory purpose: patent filing, copyright registration, R&D audit, institutional accountability, KYS disclosure, dispute resolution, defensive publication, trade-secret assessment, or public-interest review.

Compact formula:

> The corpus preserves the traces. Cogentia produces the view. Humans remain imputable.

---

## 2. Why this category is needed

AI-assisted work creates a recurring evidentiary problem.

A system may generate, recombine, draft, optimize, simulate, classify or suggest. A living human may frame the problem, select the path, reject alternatives, transform outputs, test hypotheses, claim authorship, assume responsibility, or file a patent.

The legal and ethical question is not whether AI was used.

The question is:

> Where is the real human contribution, and can it be reconstructed from governed traces?

This question appears in at least five domains:

1. **Patent-oriented R&D** — who made the inventive contribution, and how was AI involved?
2. **Copyright and creative works** — what expressive or compositional elements were human-authored?
3. **Institutional accountability** — which living humans, officers, employees or mandataries made the relevant decision?
4. **KYS disclosure** — what can a subject prove about its own contribution without exposing the whole corpus?
5. **Commons financing** — how can private AI-assisted work finance public infrastructure without becoming extractive or irresponsible?

---

## 3. Source, view, dossier, certificate

The category must not be confused with a ledger, blockchain, audit log or primary registry.

```text
Raw archive
  = undifferentiated traces, often ungoverned.

Governed corpus
  = versioned, attributable, access-controlled, contestable source material.

Human Contribution View
  = probatory cumulative derived product over the corpus.

Human Contribution Dossier
  = legally or procedurally packaged form of the view for a specific use.

Human Contribution Certificate
  = signed, attested, timestamped or audited statement derived from the dossier.
```

The view must remain source-linked. Every significant claim inside the view should be traceable back to corpus material: notes, commits, issues, prompts, model outputs, lab notebooks, experimental results, meeting minutes, decisions, annotations, or dated correspondence.

The view may reorganize the source. It may select, compress, sequence, qualify and interpret traces. But it must not become an independent source of truth detached from its evidence.

---

## 4. Definition

A Human Contribution View may be defined as follows:

> A Human Contribution View is a situated, probatory, cumulative derived product generated from a governed corpus in order to document the human contribution to an AI-assisted process, while preserving source traceability, uncertainty, contestability, access boundaries and responsibility chains.

This definition implies seven requirements.

### 4.1 Source dependence

The view derives from corpus traces. It should identify the files, commits, branches, databases, attachments, artifacts or records from which its claims are extracted.

### 4.2 Cumulative reconstruction

A contribution is usually not a single event. It is a path: problem framing, exploration, rejection, transformation, testing, claim selection, review, correction, and final decision.

The view reconstructs that path.

### 4.3 Probatory purpose

The view is built for a purpose: proving, auditing, defending, disclosing, certifying, contesting, filing, abandoning, or publishing.

### 4.4 Human imputability

The view must identify the living humans whose acts, judgments, selections, transformations or validations materially shaped the result.

### 4.5 AI role disclosure

The view should state what AI systems did: ideation, drafting, search, classification, simulation, critique, translation, synthesis, claim drafting, prior-art mapping, or workflow orchestration.

### 4.6 Contestability

The view is not sacred. It must support correction, objection, versioning, adverse review and alternative interpretations.

### 4.7 Access discipline

The view must not expose more than its purpose requires. A client, examiner, court, partner, reviewer, public body or KYS requester may receive different resolutions of the same underlying contribution history.

---

## 5. Human contribution is not mere human appearance

The category exists to prevent a specific failure mode: **human-washing**.

Human-washing occurs when an AI-generated result is formally attributed to a human who did not make a substantial contribution, merely because the legal system requires a human inventor, author, officer, reviewer or responsible person.

The opposite failure mode is **AI erasure**: pretending that a process was purely human when AI materially shaped the result.

Cogentia should reject both.

```text
AI-only output + human signature
  = fragile and potentially deceptive.

Human problem framing + AI exploration + human selection + human transformation + human validation
  = potentially legitimate, if traced.
```

A Human Contribution View does not exist to hide AI use. It exists to document the human role with enough clarity that AI use can be acknowledged without dissolving human imputability.

---

## 6. Patent-oriented use

In a patent-oriented private or restricted mode of Cogentia Commons, the Human Contribution View documents the path from initial technical problem to possible filing, secrecy, defensive publication, public source document, suspension, or abandonment.

It may include:

- the human-formulated technical problem;
- candidate solution spaces explored with AI assistance;
- prior-art search and adverse novelty objections;
- rejected variants and reasons for rejection;
- retained variants and reasons for retention;
- unexpected technical effects;
- experiments, prototypes, simulations or tests;
- claim-strategy decisions;
- named human contributors and their contribution types;
- AI tools used and their roles;
- visibility-routing decision: patent, trade secret, defensive publication, public source, restricted memo, suspension or abandonment.

Compact formula:

> Cogentia Patents does not sell automatic invention. It sells traceable human contribution in AI-assisted invention work.

---

## 7. Copyright and creative works

For creative works, the Human Contribution View may document:

- original human concept, theme, prompt strategy or compositional direction;
- human selection among machine-generated alternatives;
- editing, arrangement, transformation, translation or curation;
- expressive elements created without AI;
- expressive elements generated with AI but substantially transformed by a human;
- model outputs that remain unprotectable, uncertain or excluded;
- declarations required for copyright registration or publisher review;
- rights and moral-rights boundaries;
- use of posthumous, institutional, collective or third-party corpus material.

The goal is not to inflate protectability. The goal is to distinguish human authorship, machine output, derived transformation, and unclaimed material.

---

## 8. Institutional accountability

The same category applies beyond intellectual property.

For a legal person, public body, association, platform, company or community, the issue may not be inventorship or authorship but responsibility.

A Human Contribution View can reconstruct:

- who held the mandate;
- who made or approved the decision;
- who drafted, reviewed, signed or transmitted the act;
- what AI systems or automated workflows intervened;
- what documents or data were used;
- what objections were raised;
- what alternatives were rejected;
- what human validation anchors were required;
- what redress or appeal paths remain open.

This connects the category to Actarchia and to the doctrine of traceability of acts: a powerful organization should not dissolve responsibility into process, software, committees, subcontractors or AI systems.

Compact formula:

> The twin of a legal person traces a continuity of acts, mandates and capacities; the Human Contribution View traces the living humans inside that continuity when responsibility matters.

---

## 9. PrivaAI and KYS implications

Human Contribution Views affect PrivaAI and KYS directly.

A KYS profile should not only represent what a subject is structurally like. In some contexts, it must also represent what the subject has actually contributed, under which mandate, with which traces, and at which disclosure level.

This suggests a KYS extension:

```text
KYS Contribution Profile
  = purpose-limited disclosure of a subject's contribution history,
    derived from one or more Human Contribution Views.
```

For a natural person, the KYS Contribution Profile should protect the subject:

- prove contribution without exposing the whole corpus;
- prevent human-washing by employers, platforms or clients;
- preserve privacy, dignity, intimacy and trade secrecy;
- distinguish invention, authorship, review, supervision, selection, correction and validation;
- support objection and correction.

For a legal person, the KYS Contribution Profile should support accountability:

- who acted for the entity;
- under which mandate;
- with what AI assistance;
- with what internal or external review;
- with which conflicts of interest;
- with what responsibility chain.

PrivaAI's role is not to own these views. Its role is to govern, certify, audit, dispute-resolve, privacy-protect and prevent capture of the contribution-profile layer.

## 10. Visibility modes

Human Contribution Views may be public, private, restricted, embargoed or redacted.

```text
private corpus → internal Human Contribution View
private R&D corpus → patent dossier
private R&D corpus → trade-secret memo
private R&D corpus → defensive publication
private R&D corpus → public source document
private institutional corpus → redacted accountability view
personal corpus → KYS Contribution Profile
```

The visibility state should be explicit and revisable.

The default rule remains: Cogentia Commons is public by default and private by exception. Human Contribution Views are often born in private or restricted modes because premature disclosure may harm persons, destroy patentability, expose trade secrets, or confuse unstable traces with stabilized claims.

Private origin does not imply permanent secrecy.

## 11. Commercial use and commons financing

A paid private service is legitimate if it does not redefine the public commons around private capture.

The commercial proposition is narrow:

> Private clients pay for governed private corpora, AI-assisted exploration, contribution views, adverse review, IP routing and compliance support. That revenue finances, hardens and maintains the public Cogentia Commons infrastructure.

This applies naturally to FractaVolta because FractaVolta operates the stack where energy, compute, cognition and civic capacity meet.

Commercial AI-assisted R&D becomes one revenue path among others:

```text
AI-assisted R&D service
→ paid private mode
→ governed corpus + Human Contribution Views
→ stronger patent / secrecy / publication decisions
→ revenue
→ maintenance of public tools, independent research, local capacity, civic commons
```

The ethical constraint is explicit:

> AI may accelerate invention; it must not accelerate irresponsible enclosure of the commons.

## 13. Failure modes

| Failure mode | Description | Safeguard |
|---|---|---|
| Human-washing | A human is named but did not substantially contribute. | Contribution types, evidence, adverse review. |
| AI erasure | AI materially shaped the result but is hidden. | AI role disclosure. |
| Corpus inversion | The view becomes treated as the source. | Source-linked claims and corpus primacy. |
| Over-disclosure | The view exposes more than necessary. | Purpose-limited KYS disclosure. |
| Patent trolling acceleration | AI generates weak claims for enclosure. | Prior-art objections, abandonment and defensive-publication routing. |
| Institutional laundering | A legal person hides living decision-makers behind workflow. | Mandate and responsibility-chain tracing. |
| Private capture | Paid clients redefine the commons. | Public-by-default doctrine and explicit commons-financing boundary. |
